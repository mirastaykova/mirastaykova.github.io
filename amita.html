AMITA Health Design Challenge

October 15-16th, 2016

As part of a 30 hour design hackathon, my team of three very sleep-deprived and brilliant designers created a technical and non-technical solution to tackle the issues many older adults face when navigating the complex insurance enrollment process.

Technical: SARA, an online platform, creates an easy and pleasant process for matching older adults to the right medicare supplement for them. Using the turbotax model, this platform would collect the user's information in a conversation-like manner, and store it to simplify annual re-enrollment. Once the information is collected, SARA compares the options in plain english terms that are much more user-friendly and personalized than other web brokers.

Non-technical: The board game L.I.F.E. (life-long insurance for everyone) serves as a fun interactive tool to educate families and increase health literacy. Using a simple game model based on more familiar games such as Monopoly and the game of LIFE, the game introduces individuals to basic health care terms and trade-offs between different insurance plans. The education tool hopes to educate families, and older adults specifically, to be well-informed consumers in the health care market, and gain confidence when advocating for themselves and navigating the insurance process.
