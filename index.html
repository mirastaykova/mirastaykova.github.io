Just a nerdy powerlifter trying to be useful.

I'm a third year undergrad at Northwestern University. Recently discovered the terminology of human-centered design, which I previously referred to as "common sense". My hobbies include frequenting blues clubs, lifting heavy weights and punching bags in rusty basements (sorry mom), training others to do the same while pursuing healthy lifestyles, surrealist art, and making people smile.

I've hopped from pyschology, to advocacy, to law, to design, always finding the aspect that I love in each boil down to the same theme: people. That's when human-centered design made sense. Now I'm just searching for a way to impact people through a creative and innovative approach. I'm interested in learning more about user experience design, service design, and customer success. I'm excited to see what possibilities lie ahead!
