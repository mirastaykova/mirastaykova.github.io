    Evanston Animal Shelter (ongoing)

  • How can we improve utilization of space, organization, and separation of sick and healthy cats in the isolation room?
  • We were hired by the local animal shelter to redesign and reimagine a space within their building currently used quite inefficiently to isolate possibly sick cats and store miscellaneous necessities and dry food. Volunteers and staff struggle with the lack of space, out-of-date cages, cluttered storage, and inability to properly separate sick and healthy cats.
  • Constrained by brick walls that are difficult to install shelving in and three brick structures emerging from the ground that cannot be removed, we are currently working on brainstorming alternative floor plans and cage and storage designs to improve the usability of the room.
  • We are currently observing the shelter, interviewing staff, and researching use of space in other nearby shelters. We are also researching interior design and space-maximizing best practices in non-shelter environments to inform our ideating process.
